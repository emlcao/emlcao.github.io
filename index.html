I am Emily (Linh) Cao, a Software Development Engineer at Verizon Media. I obtained my Master's degree in Library and Information Science at the University of Illinois (UIUC). My research focused on building distributed honeypots for large-scale networks connecting national labs and universities. The system is deployed at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA) for real-time sharing of security alerts.

During my Master's training, I have accumulated:
(1) A strong theoretical foundation in information sciences with coursework in probability, statistics, and unstructured data mining, and
(2) Practical experience in operating large-scale and secure data pipelines by implementing SSH honeypots, millions-of-events-per-second peer-to-peer security intelligence, and continuous integration tools.

Research

I have participated in the following research projects.

  • Distributed Honeypots and Security Intelligence Sharing

    The SDAIA project addresses a critical need for security solutions for open science networks, such as the Science DMZ model, and addresses the special architecture of these networks through a virtual security appliance that benefits from shared intelligence to protect the site, and further provide intelligence to the wider community. The poster will present the unique architecture of SDAIA and its reliance on Apache Kafka. Apache Kafka is designed to distribute message extensively and it is easy to scale out. This system helps sharing real-time attacks from honeypot to other components in event store for quickly spreading threat among open science networks.
